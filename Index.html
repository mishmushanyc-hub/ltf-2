Guest Profile — Heidi Cannon

Heidi grew up everywhere and nowhere at the same time — moving between Hawaii, Florida, California, West Virginia, Philly, and eventually New York. Home was never a place, it was something more fluid. Music, memory, and small sensory moments stuck more than geography ever did.

Her relationship to food mirrors that. It swings between extremes — Spam and Spaghettios, sushi at six years old, diner breakfasts, anchovies on toast, caviar when she can get it. There's no strict logic, just instinct. A “freak palette,” as she puts it — where high and low, comfort and curiosity all sit at the same table. Each dish reflects a moment, a habit, or a phase, pulled from a life that's been constantly in motion but always grounded in feeling.

The Menu
Course 01

Goat Cheese & Pistachio Stuffed Figs

lil spicy, lil sweet

Figs come from an early fixation — Heidi references the Sylvia Plath “fig tree,” the idea of too many lives and not knowing which one to choose. This dish sets that tone: soft, sweet, a little sharp. It’s the starting point of everything — possibility, tension, and a slightly romanticized way of looking at the world.

Course 02

Charred Balsamic Brussels, Confit Garlic Purée

heaps and loads of parm on top

Heidi is a self-proclaimed “greens girl,” but not in a delicate way. She wants depth — char, sweetness, heaviness. This dish leans into that.

Course 03

Spam Onigiri

touch of wasabi for your nostrils

This is childhood, directly. Spam was always around, and sushi came early — introduced by parents who loved Japanese food. This dish sits right between those two worlds: something humble, something more refined, meeting in the middle. It’s also a reminder that nostalgia doesn’t have to be polished to be meaningful.

Course 04

Philly Cheesesteak Crêpe

truffle wiz for good measure

At 10 years old, Heidi spent an entire summer making thousands of crêpes, convinced she'd become a French pastry chef. Years later, she lived in Philly. This dish connects those two versions of her — obsessive creativity and lived experience. Intentionally a little absurd. That balance between serious and unserious is very much her.
